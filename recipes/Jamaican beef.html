Jamaican Beef Patties

This Jamaican beef patty recipe makes delicious curry-flavored beef pastries popular in Jamaica and other Caribbean islands.

Ingredients Pastry
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 ½ teaspoons curry powder
  • 1 pinch salt
  • ¼ cup margarine
  • ¼ cup shortening
  • ⅓ cup water
Filling
  • 2 tablespoons margarine
  • 1 small onion, finely diced
  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon pepper
  • ½ cup beef broth
  • ½ cup dry bread crumbs
  • 1 egg, beaten
Steps
  1. Preheat the oven to 400 degrees F (200 degrees C).
  2. Prepare pastry: Combine flour, curry powder, and salt in a bowl.
  3. Cut in margarine and shortening until mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  4. Stir in water until mixture forms a ball.
  5. Shape dough into a log and cut it into 10 equal pieces. Roll each piece into a 6-inch circle (approximately 1/8-inch thick) on a lightly floured surface; set aside.
  6. Prepare filling: Melt margarine in a skillet over medium heat. Sauté onion until soft and translucent, 2 to 3 minutes.
  7. Stir in ground beef; season with curry powder, thyme, salt, and pepper. Cook, stirring constantly, until beef is evenly browned, 8 to 10 minutes. Stir in beef broth and bread crumbs.
  8. Simmer until liquid is absorbed; remove from heat.
  9. Spoon equal amounts of filling onto each pastry circle. Fold over and press edges together, making a half-circle.
  10. Use the back of a fork to seal edges, then brush tops with beaten egg. Place pastries on a baking sheet.
  11. Bake in the preheated oven until golden brown, about 30 minutes.
